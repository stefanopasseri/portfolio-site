Tidal Device

Site specific installation for a canal lock in East London.

Installed beneath a footbridge in the London Docklands, this work functioned as a self-contained water cycle triggered by the tide. Canal water was pumped into a labyrinth of bags, pipes, and counterweights that slowly released it back in tiny trickles and cascades. The piece resembled a primitive machine or Rube Goldberg mechanism—absorbed moving water without serving any practical purpose. Rooted in its site, it turned the bridge’s undercroft into a sculptural stage for its fragile, provisional machinery.
